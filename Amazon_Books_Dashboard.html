💡 Revenue Insight — Q1
Total gross revenue is ₹1.26 Cr but Amazon's platform cut of 28.1% (₹35.5L) is significant. Industry standard for books is 30–40%, so this is relatively competitive. However, on high-volume titles, even a 2% reduction in the cut would add ~₹7.1L to your bottom line.
📈 Trend Insight — Q3
2023 was the peak revenue year at ₹25.6L net — 2024 declined by ~17% to ₹21.1L. This suggests possible market saturation or pricing pressure. The Jun–Aug window consistently outperforms all other months (exam prep season drives demand).
📊 Seasonal Pattern
Q1 (Jan–Mar) and Q3 (Jul–Sep) are the two dominant quarters. These align with exam notification cycles for competitive exams (UGC NET, TGT/PGT, SSC). Consider stock-up promotions in May–June to front-load demand.

💡 Category Insight — Q2
Your catalog is hyper-concentrated — just 2 categories (Competitive Exam + Physical Education) generate 92.4% of all net revenue. This is high-risk but also an opportunity: dominate these niches before diversifying.
🔍 Margin Gap — Q12 Preview
Despite high volume, the "Other" category has the lowest net/unit (₹380) but also the lowest platform cut (16.9%). This suggests books priced below Amazon's cut threshold — an opportunity to reprice upward without eroding margin %.
⚠️ Revenue Risk
With zero presence in Science, Math, History — high-demand categories for competitive exams — there's a significant untapped market. A student buying PE books likely also needs GK/Science prep materials.

🏆 Bestseller Insight — Q4
"Physical Education Universe" leads by volume at 1,250 units but only contributes ₹4.4L net — that's ₹356/unit. Meanwhile "Physical Education - UGC Net Digest" (678 units) generates ₹10.2L net — 3× the net per unit. Volume ≠ profit.
💰 Margin Insight — Q5
The UGC NET Digest 3-Vol Set earns ₹2,300/unit margin — the highest in the catalog. If you pushed 100 more units of this title, that's ₹2.3L incremental revenue. Focus marketing budget on high-margin slow-movers before price-cutting bestsellers.
📅 Consistency Insight — Q6
Books with 200+ active selling days have institutional demand — likely prescribed/recommended by institutes. These titles are immune to promotional cycles and should never be discounted. Books with 1–2 active days are one-time spike purchases — demand-volatile, discount-sensitive.
🔑 Action Item
Create two pricing strategies: premium/stable pricing for high-consistency books (steady demand, price-insensitive) and promotional/bundled pricing for low-consistency titles to convert spike demand into repeat purchases.

📊 Spike Analysis — Q9
Aug 2023 spike (₹41,075 in a single day) is 4.8× the daily average. This coincides with UGC NET exam notifications — a classic demand pulse event. Build an "exam alert" promotions calendar around known exam cycle dates to pre-position inventory and listings.

✅ Tiered Strategy Outcome — Q15
Books priced above ₹1,000 would see their cut drop from ~28.1% to 26.1%. With approximately 3,200 orders (from high-priced titles like UGC Net, hardcovers), this generates roughly ₹1.4–1.8L additional net revenue per year — without any volume change. This is a pure margin recovery play.
📋 Portfolio Pruning Recommendation — Q14
The bottom 135 books (20% of catalog) contribute only 0.3% of net revenue (₹27,300). Removing these frees up catalog management time and can redirect promotional budget. Set a rule: any title earning less than ₹500/year net gets delisted or bundled into a "combo pack" to preserve value.
📦 Bundle Upsell Strategy — Q13
The most frequently co-purchased pairs represent a natural bundle offer opportunity. A 5% bundle discount still nets higher revenue than two separate purchases if it increases attach rate by 20%+. Start with the top 3 pairs as "Study Combos" for exam candidates.
